## Company Overview

**Zenskar** is an AI-native Order-to-Cash (O2C) platform for B2B XaaS companies. It automates billing, revenue recognition, collections, and SaaS metrics for finance teams dealing with complex pricing models (usage-based, metered, enterprise contracts, hybrid billing).

- **Founders:** Apurv Bansal (CEO & CFO), Saurabh Agrawal (Co-Founder & CPTO)
- **Employees:** ~81
- **Funding:** $6.5M Seed from Bessemer Venture Partners
- **Website:** https://www.zenskar.com/

## Product — Key Features

- **Flexible Billing Automation:** Proprietary graphical data model supports any pricing — flat fee, tiered, volume, usage-based, minimums, proration, custom contracts — without developers
- **Contracts AI:** Extracts billing terms from contracts automatically
- **Usage Metering:** Ingest raw/aggregated usage via APIs, CSV, or 100+ data warehouses
- **Revenue Recognition:** ASC 606 / IFRS 15 compliant
- **Analytics & Reporting:** MRR, ARR, LTV, revenue waterfall, DSO, custom reports
- **Zen AI:** Natural language queries on financial data

**Core differentiator:** Graphical data model — any pricing a sales team describes in words can be automated without custom code. Legacy billing tools use a linear list of products + prices. Real contracts use the full flexibility of English. Zenskar handles that.

## Key Terminology

- **O2C (Order-to-Cash):** End-to-end process from signed contract to money in the bank
- **RevRec (Revenue Recognition):** When revenue is "earned" vs received — ASC 606 (US) / IFRS 15 (international)
- **Deferred Revenue:** Money received but not yet earned
- **AR (Accounts Receivable):** Money owed by customers
- **DSO (Days Sales Outstanding):** Average days to collect payment
- **MRR/ARR:** Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue
- **CPQ:** Configure, Price, Quote

## How to Analyze Data Exports

When you receive a data export from the Zenskar marketing dashboard, please:

1. **Start with the headline numbers** — total sessions, clicks, spend, conversions. What's the overall trend?
2. **Compare periods** — current vs previous. What's improving, what's declining?
3. **Channel analysis** — which channels drive the most engaged traffic? Organic vs paid efficiency.
4. **Keyword/query analysis:**
   - Separate branded (contains "zenskar") vs non-branded queries
   - Identify high-impression, low-CTR opportunities (ranking but not getting clicked)
   - Flag competitor queries and their organic positions
5. **Google Ads specific:**
   - Flag irrelevant search terms (budget waste)
   - Check Quality Scores — anything below 5/10 needs attention
   - Landing page quality — BELOW_AVERAGE is a red flag
   - Calculate effective cost per demo/conversion
6. **Cross-channel insights:**
   - Paid vs organic keyword overlap — are we paying for clicks we could get free?
   - Organic competitor coverage — which competitors have organic presence vs need paid?
7. **Actionable recommendations** — prioritized as Immediate / This Week / This Month
8. **Always consider the ICP** — US/UK/Canada traffic matters most. Focus on finance buyer intent signals.
9. **Assess landing page alignment** — do the pages match what the searcher was looking for?
10. **Flag anomalies** — unusual spend spikes, sudden rank changes, tracking gaps (e.g., "Unassigned" channel in GA4)
